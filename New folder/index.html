About this page

This brief educational site was created to illustrate how biomedical engineering contributes to breast cancer care — covering imaging, targeted therapies, surgical technology, and reconstruction. Content is introductory and intended for students and the general public; it does not replace professional medical guidance.

Advanced Imaging & Diagnostics

Engineers design and improve imaging systems—mammography, tomosynthesis (3D mammography), MRI, PET/CT—and the computational methods that analyze images (AI-based image analysis, radiomics) to detect cancer earlier and more accurately.

Mammography & Tomosynthesis

Improved detectors and reconstruction algorithms reduce false positives and improve lesion visibility in dense breast tissue.

Image Analysis (AI)

Machine learning models assist radiologists by highlighting suspicious regions and quantifying tumor characteristics.

Targeted Drug Delivery & Nanotechnology

Biomedical engineers develop drug carriers (liposomes, nanoparticles) and release systems that concentrate chemotherapy at tumor sites while reducing systemic toxicity.

Surgical Technology & Robotics

Robotic-assisted surgery, intraoperative imaging, and navigation systems enable surgeons to remove tumors more precisely while preserving healthy tissue.

Robotic Assistance

Robotic systems provide dexterity and stability for complex reconstructive procedures.

Intraoperative Imaging

Real-time imaging guides margins assessment and helps spare healthy tissue.

Tissue Engineering & Reconstruction

Engineers work on biomaterials, engineered scaffolds, and 3D-printed implants for breast reconstruction and soft-tissue regeneration.

Future Directions

  • Personalized implants and regenerative therapies using patient-derived cells
  • Improved biosensors for early detection and monitoring minimal residual disease
  • Integrated AI systems combining imaging, genomics and clinical data for tailored treatment
